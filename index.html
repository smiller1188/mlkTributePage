Dr. Martin Luther King Jr

The man who had a dream for freedom and equal rights

The Timeline to Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.'s Life:

  • 1929 - Born in Atlanta, Georgia
  • 1944 - Begins his Freshman year at Moorehouse College in Atlanta where he originally studies medicine and law but later decides on ministry
  • 1948 - Graduates from Moorehouse college with a degree in Sociology. The next three years he attends Cozer Theological Seminary in Chester, Pennsylvania. Here he learns Mahatma Ghandi's nonviolence philosophy.
  • 1951 - He is valedictorian of his class. King then attends graduate school at Boston University. He meets Coretta Scott while in Boston.
  • 1953 - He marries Coretta Scott.The following year he becomes pastor of Dexter Avenue Baptist Church in Montgomery, Alabama.
  • 1955 - He earns his Ph.D in theology from Boston University. King is chosen to be the head of the Montgomery Improvement Association, which was formed by the black community to end segregated city buses.
  • 1956 - The US Supreme Court rules that segregation on city buses is unconstitutional.
  • 1957 - Martin Luther King Jr and a group of activists established a group known as the Southern Christian Leadership Conference. It involved helping local organizations carry out civil rights in the south.
  • 1960 - King and his family move back to Atlanta where he is co-pastor with his father at Ebenezer Baptist Church.
  • 1963 - King attends a campaign to end segregation at lunch counters and hiring practices in Birmingham, Alabama. King and others were arrested. In the Birmingham jail, King writes what is known as the "Letters from Birmingham Jail". The letters explained how he will continue to stand up for what is right in a nonviolent manner.
  • 1964 - King is announced the Nobel Prize for Peace winner.
  • 1966 - King announces a drive against racial discrimination in Chicago.
  • 1968 - King is shot and killed while in Memphis, Tennessee supporting a strike by sanitation workers.

“The ultimate measure of a man is not where he stands in moments of comfort and convenience, but where he stands at times of challenge and controversy.”

--Martin Luther King Jr
